Privacy Policy

Definition of Personal Information

“Personal information” in this Policy refers to information relating to an individual that includes a name, date of birth, or other description, etc., that could be used to identify a specific individual, or that includes an individual identification code. Information that does not identify a person by itself but could identify a person when combined with other information will be treated as “personal information” to the extent such combination is possible and lawfully available.

Compliance with Laws

When handling personal information, I comply with applicable laws and regulations, including the EU/EEA General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), guidance from competent supervisory authorities (including the Hellenic Data Protection Authority), and this Policy.

Use within the Scope of Utilization Purpose

Except where the prior consent of the individual has been obtained, or where permitted by law, I handle personal information only within the scope required to provide and support my games and website, to operate online multiplayer via Epic Online Services (“EOS”), to ensure security and prevent abuse, and to respond to inquiries and legal obligations.

Acquisition of Personal Information

I do not actively collect or store personal data beyond what is necessary to operate the website and game features. Multiplayer features (accounts, sessions, matchmaking) are provided by EOS. EOS may process limited identifiers needed to make online services function (e.g., Epic Account ID, display name, IP address/region for connectivity, session/lobby IDs). In some cases I may see limited identifiers or session metadata as necessary to operate multiplayer, but I do not export, combine, sell, or use such information for marketing. If I obtain personal information from a third party and a legal obligation to check or create records arises, I will comply with such obligation.

Personal Information of Customers Under the Age of 15

I endeavor to comply with all laws and regulations applicable to the collection, storage, and use of personal information relating to customers under the age of 15. My games are not directed to children. If a child has provided personal information without parental or guardian consent, please contact me using the details below so that I can take appropriate steps.

Security Control Action

I take reasonable and appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal information against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or alteration. I aim to keep any personal information I may handle accurate and up to date within the scope of its use.

Supervision Over a Trustee / Service Providers

I may use third-party service providers within the scope required to achieve the purposes described above, including:
•Epic Online Services (for online multiplayer features).
•Hosting/CDN providers (to deliver the website).
When using service providers, I strive to ensure appropriate safeguards through contracts and other measures. If services involve processing outside the EU/EEA, I rely on appropriate transfer mechanisms (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses) where required.

Third Party Provision

Except where permitted by law, I do not provide personal information to third parties without obtaining consent. I do not sell personal data. I may disclose information where necessary to comply with laws, enforce terms, or protect rights, property, and safety.

Responding to Requests for Access, Deletion, etc.

I will respond appropriately to requests concerning personal information—such as access, correction, deletion, restriction, or portability—based on applicable laws. Because EOS may be the controller for Epic account data, you may need to use Epic’s privacy tools or contact Epic for data they control. If you contact me, I will assist in directing your request appropriately.

Strengthening of Systems, Training, Etc.

To ensure appropriate handling of personal information, I will review and improve internal practices as needed, including updating this Policy when my services or processing change (for example, if I later add my own account systems or analytics).
